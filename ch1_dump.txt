[P144] [List Paragraph] Introduction
[P145] [Normal] In today's digital era, subscription-based services have become an integral part of modern life. From streaming platforms like Netflix and Spotify to software services like Adobe Creative Cloud and Microsoft 365, consumers are increasingly relying on recurring payment models. While these services offer convenience and flexibility, they also introduce significant challenges in tracking and managing multiple subscriptions. Users often find themselves struggling to remember payment due dates, monitoring recurring expenses, and maintaining control over their financial commitments. This problem is compounded by the variety of billing cycles, payment methods, and subscription statuses that need to be managed effectively.
[P146] [Normal] Billwise is a comprehensive subscription and bill management system designed to address these challenges. It provides users with a centralized platform to track all their recurring payments, receive timely reminders before due dates, manage autopay preferences, record payment history, and analyze monthly expenses. By automating the tracking process and providing insightful analytics, Billwise empowers users to take control of their subscription expenses and make informed financial decisions.
[P158] [List Paragraph] Motivation
[P159] [Normal] The motivation behind developing Billwise stems from the growing complexity of managing multiple subscriptions in both personal and professional contexts. Several factors drive the need for such a system:
[P160] [Normal] Growing Subscription Economy: The subscription economy has grown exponentially, with the average consumer holding multiple subscriptions across various categories. According to recent studies, the average person spends over $200 per month on subscription services, making it a significant portion of their monthly budget. This proliferation of subscriptions necessitates a systematic approach to tracking and managing these recurring expenses.
[P161] [Normal] Financial Awareness: Many users are unaware of their total subscription expenses or fail to notice when prices increase. This lack of awareness can lead to overspending and unnecessary financial strain. Billwise aims to bring transparency to subscription expenses and help users make informed decisions about their recurring payments. By providing clear visualizations and breakdowns of expenses, users can better understand where their money is going.
[P162] [Normal] Missed Payments and Late Fees: Forgetting payment due dates is a common issue that results in service interruptions and late fees. These late fees can accumulate over time and negatively impact users' credit scores. By providing timely reminders, Billwise helps users avoid these penalties and maintain uninterrupted access to their essential services. The reminder system is designed to be proactive rather than reactive.
[P163] [Normal] Complexity of Billing Cycles: Subscriptions come with various billing cycles including weekly, monthly, quarterly, and yearly. Managing these different cycles manually is challenging and error-prone. Billwise automates the tracking of these cycles and provides a unified view of all upcoming payments. This automation reduces the cognitive load on users and ensures that no payment is missed due to cycle confusion.
[P164] [Normal] Financial Planning: Understanding monthly subscription expenses is crucial for effective budgeting and financial planning. Billwise provides detailed analytics and expense breakdowns to help users better understand their spending patterns and identify potential savings opportunities. The system also offers forecasting capabilities to help users plan for future expenses.
[P171] [List Paragraph] Scope
[P172] [Normal] The scope of the Billwise project encompasses the following key areas:
[P173] [Normal] Subscription Management: The system allows users to add, view, update, and delete subscriptions. Each subscription includes essential details such as name, price, billing cycle, next due date, autopay preference, and current status. Users can also categorize subscriptions and track their payment history. The subscription management module supports various operations including adding new subscriptions with validation, updating existing subscription details, and changing subscription status (active, paused, cancelled).
[P174] [Normal] Payment Tracking: Billwise records all payments made for subscriptions, including the amount paid, payment date, and payment status. This historical data enables users to track their spending patterns and verify that payments are processed correctly. The payment module also automatically updates the next due date based on the billing cycle after each payment, ensuring that the system always reflects accurate upcoming payment information.
[P175] [Normal] Reminder System: A core feature of Billwise is its intelligent reminder system that notifies users about upcoming payments. The system identifies subscriptions due within a specified timeframe (default 3 days) and provides clear notifications about due dates. It also identifies overdue subscriptions that require immediate attention. The reminder system includes features such as daily digests, upcoming reminders, and overdue alerts to ensure users never miss a payment.
[P176] [Normal] Expense Analysis: The system provides comprehensive expense analysis capabilities, including monthly expense calculations, expense trends over time, future expense forecasts, and identification of potential savings opportunities. This analytical functionality helps users optimize their subscription spending. The analysis module includes features such as expense breakdown by billing cycle, category-based analysis, historical trend visualization, and savings opportunity identification.
[P177] [Normal] User Management: Each user has their own account with personalized subscription data. The system ensures data privacy and security by implementing user-specific access controls. Users can manage their profiles and maintain their subscription information securely.
[P178] [Normal] API Development: The project includes the development of a robust RESTful API that can be consumed by various frontend applications, enabling flexibility in user interface design and deployment options. The API is designed with best practices including proper error handling, input validation, and response formatting.

[P284] [List Paragraph] Identification of Entity and Relationships
[P285] [Normal] Entities:
[P287] [Normal] 1. User
[P288] [Normal] Represents a system user who manages subscriptions
[P289] [Normal] Attributes: user_id, name, email, password, created_at
[P290] [Normal] This entity stores all personal information about system users and serves as the primary owner of subscription data
[P291] [Normal] 2. Subscription
[P292] [Normal] Represents a recurring subscription service
[P293] [Normal] Attributes: sub_id, user_id, name, price, billing_cycle, next_due_date, autopay, status, created_at
[P294] [Normal] This entity captures all details about each subscription including pricing, billing cycle, and current status
[P295] [Normal] 3. Payment
[P296] [Normal] Represents a payment transaction for a subscription
[P297] [Normal] Attributes: payment_id, sub_id, amount, payment_date, status, created_at
[P298] [Normal] This entity maintains the complete payment history for each subscription
[P299] [Normal] 4. PaymentMethod
[P300] [Normal] Represents user's payment methods (cards, bank accounts, etc.)
[P301] [Normal] Attributes: method_id, user_id, method_name, details, created_at
[P302] [Normal] This entity stores information about user's preferred payment methods for subscriptions
[P304] [Normal] Relationships:
[P306] [Normal] 1. User-Subscription Relationship
[P307] [Normal] One User can have many Subscriptions (1:M)
[P308] [Normal] Each Subscription belongs to exactly one User
[P309] [Normal] This is a one-to-many relationship with a foreign key (user_id) in Subscription table
[P310] [Normal] Ensures data isolation and proper ownership of subscription records
[P311] [Normal] 2. Subscription-Payment Relationship
[P312] [Normal] One Subscription can have many Payments (1:M)
[P313] [Normal] Each Payment belongs to exactly one Subscription
[P314] [Normal] This is a one-to-many relationship with a foreign key (sub_id) in Payment table
[P315] [Normal] Payments are automatically deleted when a subscription is deleted (cascade delete)
[P316] [Normal] Maintains complete payment history for each subscription
[P317] [Normal] 3. User-PaymentMethod Relationship
[P318] [Normal] One User can have many Payment Methods (1:M)
[P319] [Normal] Each Payment Method belongs to exactly one User
[P320] [Normal] This is a one-to-many relationship with a foreign key (user_id) in PaymentMethod table
[P321] [Normal] Allows users to store multiple payment options
[P322] [Normal] 4. Payment-Subscription Relationship
[P323] [Normal] Each payment is linked to exactly one subscription through foreign key relationship
[P324] [Normal] Payments cannot exist without a valid parent subscription
[P325] [Normal] This ensures referential integrity and prevents orphaned payment records
